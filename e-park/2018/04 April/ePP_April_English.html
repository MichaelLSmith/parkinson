Survey reveals gaps in health services available for people with Parkinson’s

Parkinson Canada, the definitive voice of the Parkinson community in Canada for over 50 years, recently conducted a survey through Ipsos Public Affairs, to better understand the challenges faced by key stakeholder groups. Findings confirm what Canadians living with Parkinson’s along with their caregivers and health care providers have faced for some time.

Predicting the risk of Parkinson’s disease

At the University of Ottawa, Dr. Michael Schlossmacher and his colleagues have created a mathematical model that may help doctors predict who will develop Parkinson’s and at what stage in their lives.

Shortening the path to a successful treatment

Alexandre Boutet, a doctoral student at the University of Toronto, is using fMRI brain imaging technology to refine the way in which deep brain stimulation (DBS) is adjusted to meet an individual patient’s needs.
