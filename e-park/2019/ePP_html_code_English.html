Recruiting the brain’s underdogs

About half of the human brain is made up of cells, known as astrocytes, which researchers used to think were like the scaffolding that holds the more important neurons together. But Natalina Salmaso knows better. She calls astrocytes the underdogs of the brain: a dynamic class of cells that are just as busy as neurons, but whose work historically went unrecognized. Her research involves revealing how these cells help our brains stay at their best.
