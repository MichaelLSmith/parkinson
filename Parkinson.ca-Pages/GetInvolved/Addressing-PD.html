cost

Economic burden of Parkinson’s disease in Canada is $1.21 billion.

Parkinson’s has one of the highest direct healthcare costs annually of any neurological disease.

45% of people with Parkinson’s and care partners in Canada report it is difficult to pay for medical expenses related to the treatment of Parkinson’s. Next to medication, exercise is the highest-used therapy for symptom management.

the plan

Parkinson Canada asks the Government of Canada to fund a plan to address Parkinson’s disease by:

  1. Investing $30 million to expand and grow the Canadian Open Parkinson Network (C-OPN). C-OPN will include: a patient registry; diagnostic database; collection of anatomical and functional measurements; and, a biobank of genetic material. The goal is to accelerate research discoveries to find new treatment options and ultimately a cure. Parkinson Canada and Brain Canada funded the first phase which got underway in 2018.
  2. Partnering with Parkinson Canada to ensure Canadians with Parkinson’s and care partners are supported through their journey by ensuring equitable, timely and affordable access to:
    • Early and accurate diagnosis;
    • Specialized, multidisciplinary care;
    • Drug treatments, exercise and other therapeutic options;
    • Care partner supports that target financial security, workplace flexibility, health and wellbeing;
    • Home and community care;
    • Long-term care;
    • Income supports (e.g. Disability Tax Credit);
    • Palliative and end-of-life care options.

    If elected, we ask that you join us in being a champion for Canadians impacted by Parkinson’s disease by investing in discoveries for tomorrow and help for today.
